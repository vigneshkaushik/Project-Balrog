## Anchor constraint

Structural slab edge is fixed in vertical position and horizontal profile. The facade system connects to the slab edge via engineered bracket/anchor systems. Misalignment between the slab as-built position and the facade design is extremely common and MUST be resolved by the adjustable bracket system — not by modifying the structure post-pour.

## Recommended resolutions (ranked)

### 1. 3-axis adjustable bracket system to absorb tolerance

**Effort:** Low.

All curtain wall and unitized facade systems must have a 3-axis adjustable anchor/bracket system (horizontal, vertical, in/out) sized to absorb: slab edge construction tolerance (±20mm typical RC), structural deflection under full load (SE to supply value), facade thermal movement (calculated from temperature differential), and any slab camber. Typical required adjustment range: ±30mm all axes. Facade engineer must confirm bracket design covers the worst-case combination of all tolerances.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Facade engineer confirms bracket adjustment range against SE-supplied tolerance and deflection values
- [ ] As-built slab survey must be completed on all floors before facade installation begins — deviations exceeding bracket range must be identified
- [ ] Bracket capacity at adjusted positions checked (eccentric load calculation)

### 2. Pre-installation as-built slab survey and facade setout reconciliation

**Effort:** High. **VO risk:** Yes (formal variation / redesign likely).

Conduct a full laser scan or total station survey of the slab edges on all floors before facade installation begins. Compare as-built to design and identify floors where bracket adjustment alone is insufficient. For these floors, the facade setout must be revised and panels re-dimensioned. Factor revised fabrication time into the programme before proceeding.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Survey tolerance report reviewed by facade engineer
- [ ] Panel re-dimensioning (where required) factored into revised fabrication programme
- [ ] Structural engineer reviews any slab edges with significant deviation (>30mm) to understand cause

### 3. Concrete packing or grinding at bracket fixing zone

**Effort:** Medium.

For localised slab edge irregularities at bracket fixing locations (honeycombing, low spots, high spots): apply non-shrink epoxy grout packing to build up to design level, or grind high spots to design level. SE approval required for all remedial work. Packing must cure to specified strength before bracket is loaded.

**Checks:**

- [ ] SE approval for extent and material of packing/grinding
- [ ] Packing cured to specified strength before bracket is installed and loaded
- [ ] Extent of honeycombing assessed before committing to pack vs. replace approach

## Validate before acting

- [ ] Request SE-supplied slab edge tolerance and deflection data before facade bracket design is finalised — this data drives the bracket adjustment range
- [ ] Confirm slab construction sequence relative to facade installation — creep and shrinkage deflections occurring post-facade installation must be in the SE-supplied deflection values
- [ ] Confirm anchor type against slab concrete specification — post-installed anchors have different capacities in lightweight vs. normal weight vs. high-strength concrete

## Escalate immediately if

- Slab as-built deviation exceeds 50mm from design — structural investigation required before facade installation proceeds
- Facade bracket fixing location lands in a PT tendon anchor zone or at a post-installed anchor cluster — SE must confirm fixing is permissible

## Downstream risk if unresolved

Facade installation that proceeds without a reconciled as-built survey produces cumulative panel misalignments that are visible on the completed building exterior. Re-installation of unitized facade panels costs 3–5× the original installation cost. Survey first, install second — no exceptions.
