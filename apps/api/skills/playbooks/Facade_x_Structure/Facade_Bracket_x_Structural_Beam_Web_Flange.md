## Anchor constraint

Structural beam properties govern the anchor capacity at each fixing location. Facade bracket positions are driven by the facade module — but must match the structural fixing zones approved by the SE.

## Recommended resolutions (ranked)

### 1. Design facade bracket geometry to suit structural connection zone

**Effort:** Medium.

Facade anchor brackets must be designed to suit the available connection geometry — top of slab, slab edge, beam top flange, or beam web. For unitized systems, the vertical bracket interval must match floor-to-floor height, and horizontal anchor positions must land in SE-approved zones. Provide SE with full anchor load data (dead, wind, live, seismic, thermal) for capacity confirmation. SE issues a fixing zone drawing marking approved anchor locations.

**Checks:**

- [ ] SE to confirm anchor capacity at proposed fixing locations with design loads
- [ ] Facade engineer confirms bracket design matches structural connection geometry
- [ ] Anchor spacing meets facade system structural requirements

### 2. Embedded cast-in anchor channels or plates (preferred for high-load brackets)

**Effort:** Medium.

For high-load facade brackets, specify cast-in anchor channels (e.g., Halfen, Jordahl, or approved equivalent) or embedded plates in the slab or beam top flange. Cast-in systems provide higher and more predictable capacity than post-installed anchors and eliminate drilling in completed structure. Cast-in items are placed in the formwork before the pour — requires facade anchor layout to be finalised before the concrete pour date.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Facade anchor layout finalised before concrete pour date — no exceptions
- [ ] SE specifies cast-in channel/plate type and capacity
- [ ] Any displaced reinforcement at embedded plate zones approved by SE and replaced if necessary

## Validate before acting

- [ ] Obtain structural beam layout and slab thickness drawings before facade anchor drawing is prepared
- [ ] Confirm slab and beam concrete specification — post-installed anchor capacity varies significantly
- [ ] Identify any PT tendons or pre-cast elements in the slab/beam at proposed anchor locations before specifying post-installed anchors

## Escalate immediately if

- Facade anchor loads exceed slab capacity at multiple points — structural engineer must design slab strengthening or specify additional embedded elements before facade can proceed

## Downstream risk if unresolved

Facade anchor positions finalised before structural slab details are confirmed produce field adjustments that compromise bracket geometry and panel alignment. The facade anchor layout must be a collaborative design output between structural and facade engineers, not a unilateral facade assumption.
