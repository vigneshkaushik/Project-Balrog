## Anchor constraint

Structural column is immovable. Joinery is the flexible element, but it has been designed to a specific spatial layout and aesthetic. The resolution should produce a clean design outcome — not a compromised joinery unit with an awkward notch.

## Recommended resolutions (ranked)

### 1. Design joinery unit with a column pocket (column wrap)

**Effort:** Low.

Integrate the column into the joinery design — design the unit with a recessed "column pocket" to accommodate the column cross-section. Maintain minimum 25mm clearance from structural column face to joinery inner face (fire protection build-up + thermal movement tolerance). If the column is within a joinery run (e.g., kitchen or workspace), the pocket zone becomes a natural transition between units. In display or retail joinery, the column can be cased in the same finish material as the joinery for a seamless appearance.

**Checks:**

- [ ] 25mm min clearance from structural column face to joinery inner face
- [ ] Fire protection build-up on column not compromised by joinery contact
- [ ] Joinery units either side of column pocket are independently supported — not hung from or bearing on the column

### 2. Column as joinery design anchor / feature element

**Effort:** Low.

Use the column as a compositional anchor in the joinery design — flank the column with two joinery units, use the column face as a display surface or backing board, or design the column casing as a material highlight within the joinery composition. This approach often produces superior design outcomes to hiding the column.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Interior designer approval for column-as-feature treatment
- [ ] Column finish/casing material spec confirmed (fire protection maintained)
- [ ] Column casing material does not compromise the fire rating of the column protection system

## Validate before acting

- [ ] Confirm column dimensions AND fire protection build-up before joinery design is finalised — the effective column dimension includes the fire protection
- [ ] Check if column has a separate architectural casing specified separately — this affects the total effective dimension the joinery must accommodate
- [ ] Identify any MEP conduits or pipes running in the joinery — column presence affects their route within the joinery unit

## Escalate immediately if

- Joinery is already fabricated and cannot accommodate the column without structural modification — re-fabrication required at significant cost

## Downstream risk if unresolved

Built-in joinery around columns that has not been dimensionally coordinated with the as-built column position (including fire protection) will not fit on site. Always verify column position from as-built survey before issuing joinery fabrication drawings. Column as-built positions can vary ±20mm from grid.
