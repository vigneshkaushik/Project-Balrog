## Anchor constraint

Structural upstands, kerbs, slab penetrations, and drainage outlets are fixed in position. Raised floor pedestal grid must accommodate them without compromising floor levelness or access.

## Recommended resolutions (ranked)

### 1. Adjust pedestal grid around fixed slab features

**Effort:** Low.

Raised access floor pedestal grids are adjustable in layout. Obtain a slab as-built survey showing all upstands, kerbs, penetrations, and drainage outlets before the raised floor layout is drawn. Adjust the pedestal grid to avoid these elements — smaller corner pedestals or bespoke pedestal heights are used to transition around obstacles. Levelness tolerance must be maintained: ±1.5mm over any 3m length.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Slab survey completed before raised floor layout is drawn
- [ ] Levelness tolerance maintained across all adjusted pedestal zones (±1.5mm/3m)
- [ ] Drainage outlets accessible above raised floor — cut-out tiles or access tiles specified at each drainage position

### 2. Bridging panel over slab penetrations

**Effort:** Medium.

Where a slab penetration falls within the raised floor field, design a bridging structural tile with a formed cut-out and removable grommet or access cover. The bridging tile spans the penetration without a pedestal. Confirm structural tile spanning capacity for the clear span. Ensure fire stopping at the penetration is maintained below the raised floor.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Structural tile spanning capacity confirmed for the clear span dimension
- [ ] Penetration access maintained (removable grommet or access tile)
- [ ] Fire stopping at penetration maintained below raised floor level

## Validate before acting

- [ ] Complete slab as-built survey before raised floor layout is produced — without this the layout is guesswork
- [ ] Confirm all slab penetrations and drainage positions are finalised before raised floor specification is issued
- [ ] Check if underfloor services (cables, pipes) below raised floor need access doors in the raised floor — this affects pedestal grid layout in those zones

## Escalate immediately if

- Survey reveals slab features or deviations that exceed manufacturer-adjustable pedestal range or structural tile span tables — involve structural engineer and raised-floor specialist before ordering materials.

## Downstream risk if unresolved

Raised floor pedestals installed without a slab survey on a slab with significant upstands and penetrations will produce an uneven floor and missing panel supports. Survey before layout. Layout before installation. Never the other way around.
