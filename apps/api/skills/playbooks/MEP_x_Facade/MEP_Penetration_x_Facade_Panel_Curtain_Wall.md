## Anchor constraint

Facade systems are weather-critical and structurally engineered. MEP penetrations through facade must be weatherproof, airtight, and must not compromise thermal, structural, or acoustic performance of the facade system. No penetration is trivial in a unitized or high-performance facade.

## Recommended resolutions (ranked)

### 1. Pre-formed sleeve with weatherproof collar (pre-fabrication)

**Effort:** Medium.

All MEP penetrations through facade panels must be pre-formed before panel fabrication or installation. Specify: galvanised steel sleeve (stainless in coastal environments), EPDM/neoprene gasket, and compatible silicone sealant on the exterior face. Sleeve must provide 3mm all-round gap between MEP service and sleeve for differential movement. Minimum 75mm sleeve depth. If the service is a cold pipe or duct, insulation must continue through the sleeve to prevent thermal bridging and condensation within the sleeve.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Facade engineer to design and approve sleeve detail
- [ ] Sealant compatibility confirmed with facade system silicone spec
- [ ] 3mm all-round movement gap maintained between service and sleeve
- [ ] Cold service insulation continuous through sleeve — no thermal bridge
- [ ] Penetration not located on panel-to-panel joint — panels must be positioned accordingly

### 2. Facade-integrated spigot (for small services)

**Effort:** Medium.

For small conduits or pipes (≤DN50), terminate the internal MEP at the inner facade face with a flanged connection, and connect to a facade-integrated spigot or cover plate installed by the facade subcontractor as part of their package. The interface between MEP contractor and facade contractor must be clearly defined in the contract scope and on the facade shop drawing.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Contract scope interface between MEP and facade contractor clearly defined and agreed
- [ ] External service material is suitable for exterior exposure (UV, salt, moisture)
- [ ] Connection point is accessible for maintenance

## Validate before acting

- [ ] Confirm penetration location does not land on a panel-to-panel joint, mullion, or transom
- [ ] Confirm facade system performance spec (air permeability class, water tightness class) — penetration must not compromise these
- [ ] Check if facade has a drainage cavity — penetration must not block the cavity drainage plane

## Escalate immediately if

- MEP penetration lands on a structural mullion or transom — structural implications must be assessed by facade engineer
- Penetration is through a glass unit — glass replacement or special drilled glass unit required; very expensive and long lead time

## Downstream risk if unresolved

Poorly detailed facade penetrations are a leading cause of building envelope water ingress claims. These details must be reviewed by the facade engineer and must be included in the QA inspection plan at installation. Inspect every penetration before cladding is completed — inaccessible penetrations discovered later require panel removal.
