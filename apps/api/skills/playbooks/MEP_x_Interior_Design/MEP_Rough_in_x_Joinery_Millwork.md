## Anchor constraint

Millwork and joinery positions are governed by interior design and are usually custom-fabricated with 8–16 week lead times. MEP rough-in positions (outlet boxes, conduit drop points, pipe penetrations) are flexible during design stage but become fixed once concrete is poured or drywall is closed. Coordinate before either is fixed.

## Recommended resolutions (ranked)

### 1. Overlay MEP rough-in against joinery shop drawings and adjust before fabrication

**Effort:** Low.

The most effective resolution: once joinery shop drawings are issued, overlay the MEP rough-in position drawing and adjust MEP outlet positions to suit. MEP outlets (power, data, gas drops, A/V, USB) can typically shift up to 300mm in any direction without significant redesign cost. Issue a single coordinated millwork + MEP rough-in drawing with exact positions of every outlet relative to every joinery element.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Min 50mm setback from joinery edge to nearest outlet/switch plate face confirmed
- [ ] Accessibility to MEP outlets when joinery is installed and in closed position confirmed
- [ ] If joinery has integrated MEP (in-drawer power, cable management): bespoke MEP rough-in required — detail on joint shop drawing

### 2. Integrate MEP into joinery design as a designed feature

**Effort:** Medium.

For premium interiors: design MEP outlets INTO the joinery — pop-up power modules in kitchen islands, recessed data/AV panels flush in cabinetry, floor boxes integrated into joinery platform bases. This requires MEP and joinery designers to collaborate on a single coordinated shop drawing. All integrated MEP must be accessible for maintenance without full joinery disassembly. Electrical inspector must confirm in-joinery installation meets code.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Integrated MEP is accessible for maintenance without disassembly
- [ ] Electrical code compliance confirmed for in-joinery installation (ventilation, clearances, ratings)
- [ ] Load-bearing capacity of joinery panel at MEP cut-out confirmed with joinery manufacturer

### 3. Adjust joinery layout to accommodate fixed MEP rough-in

**Effort:** Medium. **VO risk:** Yes (formal variation / redesign likely).

Only if MEP rough-in is already fixed in concrete or closed walls. Adjust the joinery layout to accommodate. Quantify dimensional impact on room layout. Confirm adjusted joinery still meets the design intent and space planning requirements.

**Checks:**

- [ ] Interior designer approval for revised joinery layout
- [ ] Revised joinery layout does not affect room space plan or circulation ergonomics
- [ ] Check adjusted joinery for new clashes with other fixed MEP services

## Validate before acting

- [ ] Request joinery shop drawings before MEP rough-in positions are finalised
- [ ] Identify all joinery items with integrated or adjacent MEP requirements: kitchen, bathroom, AV wall, reception desk, hospitality counters
- [ ] Confirm MEP rough-in programme relative to joinery fabrication lead time — if fabrication has started, positions are locked

## Escalate immediately if

- MEP conduits are already cast in concrete at positions conflicting with joinery — only option is core-drilling, which must be assessed against structural drawings
- Clash is in a bathroom — waterproof membrane, tile, and MEP rough-in sequencing must all be re-coordinated, not just the MEP position

## Downstream risk if unresolved

Joinery fabricated without MEP coordination will not fit on site. Re-fabrication costs are significant and programme impacts are critical. Ensure coordination happens before joinery shop drawing approval — not after. This is one of the most common and most expensive coordination failures in high-end commercial and hospitality fitout.
